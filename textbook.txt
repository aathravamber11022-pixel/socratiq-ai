Theme IV: Beginnings of Civilisation
The Stone Age: The Earliest People
Early man was a hunter-gatherer. It means that he moved from place to place in search of food. He depended on nature for all his needs. Early man was also a nomad. He travelled great distances to find food. He fished, hunted for wild animals, and gathered berries, nuts, wild roots and fruits.

The Stone Age is divided into three periods:
1. Palaeolithic Period (Old Stone Age): Began about 2 million years ago and lasted till about 10,000 BCE. The climate was very cold (Ice Age). Hand axes, scrapers, and borers were used.
2. Mesolithic Period (Middle Stone Age): Lasted from 10,000 BCE till 8000 BCE. People used tiny stone tools called microliths. They gradually started domesticating animals like goats, sheep, and cattle. Notable sites in India include Bhimbetka, Bagor, Tilwara, and Langhnaj.
3. Neolithic Period (New Stone Age): Began around 8000 BCE and lasted till about 4000 BCE. Marked by the Neolithic Revolution—a major shift from food gathering to food producing (agriculture). 

Major Achievements of the Neolithic Period:
- Development of Agriculture: The first cereals grown were wheat and barley.
- Domestication of Animals: The first domesticated animal was the dog.
- Pottery: Made clay pottery using the potter's wheel to store food grains and water.
- Invention of the Wheel: Made transportation faster, led to the invention of carts, and allowed weaving of clothes using spindle whorls.
- Polished Tools: Tools included sickles, hoes, mortars, and pestles for grinding grains.
